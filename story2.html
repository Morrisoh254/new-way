The Testimony of

Dora Obwaka.

This is the story of Dora Obwaka. — a testimony of redemption, restoration, and the light that overcomes darkness. Read prayerfully and be encouraged.

Dora Obwaka is widely known as a Jesus Girl—an entrepreneur, a servant of Jesus Christ, and the founder of Jesus Girls Closet. Today, she lives a life grounded in faith, purpose, and gratitude. She runs her clothing business while also supporting her mother in rehabilitation center responsibilities, helping individuals battling addiction and brokenness—struggles she once personally endured. Dora deeply values her family and every opportunity life offers her, because she understands something many never experience: She was almost gone.

Behind her vibrant personality and hopeful outlook lies a story of survival. Many see her enthusiasm today, but few know that her positivity rose from years of darkness, addiction, and internal conflict. Even during her lowest moments, some people still saw something different in her—a spiritual spark that refused to die. Though her physical life seemed lost, her spirit carried a calling waiting to be rediscovered.

The name “Jesus Girl” emerged after her rehabilitation journey—not from supernatural visions, but from a personal realization that true identity comes from carrying Jesus in one’s heart rather than merely claiming a title. She came to believe one enduring truth: Rebuilding is a lifelong process, not a one-time event.

Dora's History

Dora grew up in church and actively served, yet she lived a double life. While maintaining a Christian appearance, she struggled with partying, rebellion, and destructive habits outside church walls.

At seventeen, she took her first drink and began experimenting with drugs. Slowly, church became distant as addiction took control. Influenced by unhealthy relationships and environments, alcohol turned into a daily dependency, soon accompanied by cigarettes, weed, and shisha.

What began as experimentation evolved into identity. She openly embraced substance use, developing a careless attitude toward consequences.

At nineteen, she involuntarily entered rehabilitation for the first time.After 3 months its sad to say that the rehabilitation did not help her becouse her heart was far off from wanting change. For nearly six years, addiction dominated her life.

Her lowest moment came when she consumed spiked marijuana containing traces of heroin, pushing her deeper into physical and emotional deterioration. Despite her parents’ relentless efforts—even relocating opportunities abroad—she remained trapped.

Dora was lost

Breaking point

The breaking point arrived when her mother found her lying by the roadside, barely recognizable. In desperation, her mother cried out to God. Soon afterward, Dora chose recovery and joined Teen Challenge(A faith-based rehabilitation facility), marking the beginning of genuine transformation.

The early stages were painful. Withdrawal symptoms tested her strength daily, but prayer became her anchor. Slowly, healing began—not only physically, but spiritually.She even said,

Rebuilding is a life long process not a one time phenomenon.
During this period, Dora realized her survival carried purpose. She understood that her life was not merely being restored—it was being redirected.

Her journey has included setbacks, including relapses after rehabilitation. Yet each return strengthened her understanding that transformation is a continuous journey rather than a single moment of victory.

Today, Dora walks a path of faith, recovery, and service—turning past pain into hope for others still searching for freedom.
